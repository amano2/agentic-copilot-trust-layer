POLICY DOCUMENT: CLAIM FILING DEADLINES AND LATE FILING WAIVERS (SEC-106)
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Applicability: All Personal Lines and Commercial Policies

1. FILING DEADLINE
All claims must be submitted to the Company within thirty (30) calendar days from the date of the incident or discovery of the loss. 

2. CONSEQUENCES OF LATE FILING
Claims filed after the 30-day deadline will be subject to strict scrutiny. If the delay hinders the Company's ability to investigate the loss, inspect physical damage, or interview witnesses, the claim will be denied.

3. WAIVERS AND EXCEPTIONS
The 30-day filing window may be waived under the following circumstances:
- The insured was hospitalized or suffered severe medical incapacitation during the 30-day period (medical documentation required).
- Natural disasters or official state declarations of emergency prevented communication (subject to verification).
- The loss was not immediately discoverable, and the claim was filed within 7 days of the actual discovery.
- Any late filing exception MUST be routed for human manager approval.
