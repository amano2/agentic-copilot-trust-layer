POLICY DOCUMENT: PROPERTY FIRE DAMAGE AND ADDITIONAL LIVING EXPENSES (SEC-104)
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Applicability: Homeowners and Commercial Property Policies

1. COVERED LOSSES
- Loss or damage to the insured dwelling and personal property caused by fire, smoke, explosion, or lightning.
- Additional Living Expenses (ALE): If the insured dwelling becomes uninhabitable due to fire damage, the Company will cover necessary increase in living expenses (e.g., hotel accommodation, restaurant dining) to maintain the insured's normal standard of living.

2. ALE LIMITATIONS
- Additional Living Expenses are capped at a maximum of $5,000 or 20% of the dwelling coverage limit, whichever is lower, unless otherwise specified.
- Lodging expenses must be documented with receipts and are limited to a maximum duration of 30 days from the date of the fire.

3. EXCLUSIONS AND CONDITIONS
- Fire caused by arson or intentional act of the insured is strictly excluded.
- The property must undergo a fire marshal or safety inspection. A copy of the fire marshal's report must be made available to the claims adjuster.
